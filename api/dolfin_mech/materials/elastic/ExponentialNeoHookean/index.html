dolfin_mech.materials.elastic.ExponentialNeoHookean

Exponential Neo-Hookean Material Module.

This module defines the exponential-based Neo-Hookean hyperelastic model, characterized by its non-linear stiffening under finite strains.

Module Contents

class dolfin_mech.materials.elastic.ExponentialNeoHookean.ExponentialNeoHookean(kinematics, parameters)

Bases: dolfin_mech.materials.elastic.Elastic.ElasticMaterial

Class implementing an exponential-based Neo-Hookean elastic material model.

This constitutive model is designed for finite strain elasticity and features an exponential strain energy density function. It is often used to capture the non-linear stiffening behavior of soft materials.

The strain energy density \(\Psi\) is defined as:

\[\Psi = \frac{\beta_1}{2\beta_2\alpha} \left( e^{\beta_2 (I_C - d - 2\ln J)^\alpha} - 1 \right) + \beta_3 (I_C - d - 2\ln J) + \beta_4 (J^2 - 1 - 2\ln J)\]

where \(d\) is the spatial dimension.

Variables:

  • kinematics (Kinematics) – Kinematic quantities (F, J, C, etc.).

  • alpha (beta1, beta2, beta3, beta4,) – Material parameters.

  • Psi (ufl.Form) – Strain energy density function.

  • Sigma (ufl.Form) – Second Piola-Kirchhoff stress tensor \(\mathbf{S}\).

  • P (ufl.Form) – First Piola-Kirchhoff stress tensor \(\mathbf{P} = \mathbf{F}\mathbf{S}\).

  • sigma (ufl.Form) – Cauchy stress tensor \(\boldsymbol{\sigma} = J^{-1} \mathbf{P}\mathbf{F}^T\).
