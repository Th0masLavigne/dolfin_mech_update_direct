dolfin_mech.problems.problem_hyperelasticity_poro

Defines the PoroHyperelasticity class.

High-level manager for biphasic finite strain simulations. It models the coupled mechanics of a deformable solid skeleton and a fluid phase, orchestrating energy contributions from skeleton elasticity, mixture bulk compressibility, and pore-level fluid interactions.

General problem class for finite strain poro-hyperelasticity.

This class models a biphasic continuum consisting of a deformable solid skeleton and a fluid phase. It is designed to handle large deformations where the porosity evolves as a function of the volume change (Jacobian).

The constitutive behavior is typically split into three additive contributions to the strain energy density \(\Psi\):

\[\Psi = \Psi_{skel}(\mathbf{C}) + \Psi_{bulk}(J, \phi_s) + \Psi_{pore}(\phi_f)\]
where:

  • \(\Psi_{skel}\): Elastic energy of the dry solid skeleton.

  • \(\Psi_{bulk}\): Compressibility energy of the mixture.

  • \(\Psi_{pore}\): Energy contributions from pore pressure or fluid interactions.

Porosity Formulations: The class handles two porosity descriptors:

  1. Spatial Porosity (\(\phi_s\)): Solid volume fraction in the current configuration.

  2. Lagrangian Porosity (\(\Phi_{s0}\)): Solid volume fraction referred to the reference volume.

They are linked via the mass conservation constraint: \(\phi_s = J^{-1} \Phi_{s0}\).

add_global_stress_qois(stress_type='cauchy')[source]

Registers component-wise global stress QOIs (integrated over domain).

It decomposes the total stress into:

\[\sigma_{tot} = \sigma_{skel} + \sigma_{bulk}\]

where \(\sigma_{bulk}\) is typically related to the fluid/pore pressure contribution.

Parameters:

stress_type – “Cauchy” (spatial, \(\sigma\)) or “Piola”/”PK2” (material, \(\Sigma\)).
