dolfin_mech.operators.poro.wskel

Defines the Wskel and InverseWskel operators.

Model the internal virtual work of the solid lung skeleton (fiber network) by coupling structural elasticity with porosity-based material scaling in both finite and small strain frameworks.

Module Contents

class dolfin_mech.operators.poro.wskel.Wskel(kinematics, U_test, material_parameters, material_scaling, Phis0, measure)

Bases: dolfin_mech.operators.operator.Operator

Operator representing the solid skeleton energy contribution in a finite strain poromechanical framework.

This operator models the mechanical response of the solid skeleton (typically the elastin and collagen fiber network in lung tissue). It assembles the internal virtual work using the Second Piola-Kirchhoff stress tensor \(\mathbf{\Sigma}\) provided by a scaled porous material model.

The internal virtual work \(\delta \Pi_{int}\) is:

\[\delta \Pi_{int} = \int_{\Omega_0} \mathbf{\Sigma} : \delta \mathbf{E} \, d\Omega_0\]

where \(\mathbf{\Sigma}\) is the Second Piola-Kirchhoff stress and \(\delta \mathbf{E}\) is the virtual Green-Lagrange strain.

Variables:

  • kinematics (Kinematics) – Finite strain kinematics object.

  • solid_material (WskelLungElasticMaterial) – Underlying lung skeleton material.

  • material (PorousElasticMaterial) – Porous wrapper handling material scaling based on solid volume fraction.

  • measure (dolfin.Measure) – Integration measure (typically dx).

  • res_form (UFL form) – The resulting residual variational form.

class dolfin_mech.operators.poro.wskel.InverseWskel(kinematics, u_test, material_parameters, material_scaling, phis0, measure)

Bases: dolfin_mech.operators.operator.Operator

Operator representing the solid skeleton energy contribution in a small strain or inverse poromechanical framework.

This version is used when the configuration is assumed to be linearized or when volume fractions \(\phi_{s0}\) are provided as spatial-like quantities requiring Jacobian scaling. It uses the Cauchy stress tensor \(\mathbf{\sigma}\).

The internal virtual work \(\delta \Pi_{int}\) is:

\[\delta \Pi_{int} = \int_{\Omega} \mathbf{\sigma} : \delta \mathbf{\epsilon} \, d\Omega\]

where \(\delta \mathbf{\epsilon}\) is the virtual infinitesimal strain.

Variables:

res_form (UFL form) – The resulting residual variational form.
