dolfin_mech.operators.penalty.directionaldisplacement

Defines the DirectionalDisplacement class.

Implements a variational penalty term to weakly constrain or stabilize displacements along a prescribed direction, facilitating rigid body motion suppression and flexible Dirichlet-like boundary conditions.

Module Contents

class dolfin_mech.operators.penalty.directionaldisplacement.DirectionalDisplacement(U, U_test, measure, N_val=None, N_ini=None, N_fin=None, pen_val=None, pen_ini=None, pen_fin=None)

Bases: dolfin_mech.operators.operator.Operator

Operator representing a directional displacement penalty term.

This operator assembles a penalty energy functional that penalizes displacement along a specific direction \(\mathbf{N}\). It is typically used to weakly enforce Dirichlet-like conditions or to stabilize a model against rigid body translations in a specific orientation without using hard constraints.

The penalty potential \(\Pi_{pen}\) is defined as:

\[\Pi_{pen} = \int_{\Omega} \frac{k_{pen}}{2} (\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{N})^2 \, d\Omega\]

The resulting residual form is the first variation of this potential with respect to the displacement \(\mathbf{u}\):

\[\delta \Pi_{pen} = \int_{\Omega} k_{pen} (\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{N}) (\delta \mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{N}) \, d\Omega\]
Variables:

  • measure (dolfin.Measure) – The integration measure (typically dx or ds).

  • tv_N (TimeVaryingConstant) – Time-varying unit vector \(\mathbf{N}\) defining the penalty direction.

  • tv_pen (TimeVaryingConstant) – Time-varying penalty stiffness \(k_{pen}\).

  • res_form (UFL form) – The resulting residual variational form.
