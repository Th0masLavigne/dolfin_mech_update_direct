.. py:class:: LinearizedKinematics(u, u_old=None)

   Class to compute and store kinematic quantities for linearized solid mechanics.

   This class computes the infinitesimal strain tensor :math:`\boldsymbol{\varepsilon}`
   based on the displacement field :math:`\mathbf{u}` under the assumption of
   small displacement gradients. It provides access to the full, spherical,
   and deviatoric parts of the strain.

   :Parameters: * **u** (:py:class:`dolfin.Function`) -- Current displacement field.
                * **u_old** (:py:class:`dolfin.Function, optional`) -- Displacement field from the previous
                  time step. Defaults to None.

   :ivar u: The displacement field.
   :vartype u: :py:class:`dolfin.Function`
   :ivar dim: Spatial dimension.
   :vartype dim: :py:class:`int`
   :ivar I: Identity tensor of dimension ``dim``.
   :vartype I: :py:class:`dolfin.Identity`
   :ivar epsilon: Symmetric infinitesimal strain tensor
                  :math:`\boldsymbol{\varepsilon} = \frac{1}{2}(\nabla \mathbf{u} + \nabla \mathbf{u}^T)`.
   :vartype epsilon: :py:class:`ufl.Form`
   :ivar epsilon_sph: Spherical part of the strain tensor
                      :math:`\boldsymbol{\varepsilon}_{sph} = \frac{1}{d} \text{tr}(\boldsymbol{\varepsilon}) \mathbf{I}`.
   :vartype epsilon_sph: :py:class:`ufl.Form`
   :ivar epsilon_dev: Deviatoric part of the strain tensor
                      :math:`\boldsymbol{\varepsilon}_{dev} = \boldsymbol{\varepsilon} - \boldsymbol{\varepsilon}_{sph}`.

   :vartype epsilon_dev: :py:class:`ufl.Form`
